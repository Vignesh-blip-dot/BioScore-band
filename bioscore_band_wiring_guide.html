⚠ Strapping Pin Warning: GPIO0 is a boot strapping pin. Do NOT connect it to sensors, ADC inputs, or pull it LOW during power-up — this holds the chip in download mode. Always use GPIO2/3 for ADC reads.

3. I2C Bus — Shared by All Three Devices

The OLED, MAX30102, and MPU-6050 all share the same I2C bus. Each device has a unique address so they don't conflict.

SignalESP32-C3 GPIOAll three devices connect here
SDA GPIO 8 SDA pin of OLED + MAX30102 + MPU-6050 (all in parallel)
SCL GPIO 9 SCL pin of OLED + MAX30102 + MPU-6050 (all in parallel)
VCC 3.3 V Power all three modules from the 3V3 pin
GND GND Common ground for all modules
ℹ I2C Addresses: SSD1306 = 0x3C, MAX30102 = 0x57, MPU-6050 = 0x68 (AD0 low) or 0x69 (AD0 high). No conflicts.

4.4 — Battery Voltage Divider

The ESP32-C3 ADC reads max ~3.9 V with ADC_11db attenuation. A LiPo reaches 4.2 V when full, so we use a 1:1 voltage divider (two equal resistors) to halve the voltage before the ADC pin.

LiPo (+) ──┬── 100kΩ ──┬── 100kΩ ── GND │ │ │ GPIO 2 (ADC input + 100 nF cap to GND) │ (Also feeds ESP32-C3 BAT pin / charge circuit)
ConnectionDetails
LiPo positive (+)Top of R1 (100 kΩ)
Junction R1–R2→ GPIO 2 and one leg of 100 nF cap
Bottom of R2 + cap→ GND
LiPo negative (–)→ GND
ℹ Why two equal resistors? At 4.2 V full charge, the divider outputs 2.1 V — well within the ADC's 3.9 V range. The firmware multiplies the reading by 2 to recover the real voltage. The 100 nF cap filters ADC sampling noise.

6. Arduino IDE Setup

1
Add ESP32 board support. Open Arduino IDE → Preferences → Additional Boards Manager URLs and add:
https://raw.githubusercontent.com/espressif/arduino-esp32/gh-pages/package_esp32_index.json
2
Install ESP32 boards. Go to Tools → Board → Boards Manager → search "esp32" by Espressif → Install version 2.0.14 or later.
3
Select board: Tools → Board → ESP32 Arduino → ESP32C3 Dev Module
4
Board settings:
USB CDC On Boot: Enabled
CPU Frequency: 80 MHz (saves power)
Flash Size: 4MB
Partition Scheme: Default 4MB
5
Install required libraries via Library Manager (Tools → Manage Libraries):
  • SparkFun MAX3010x Pulse and Proximity Sensor Library
  • MPU6050 by Electronic Cats (or Jeff Rowberg's I2Cdev)
  • Adafruit SSD1306
  • Adafruit GFX Library
  • ESP32 BLE Arduino — bundled with ESP32 board package
6
Upload the firmware. Hold the BOOT button while pressing RESET on the C3 Super Mini to enter download mode, then click Upload.

10. Assembly Tips for Wearable Form Factor

1
Use flexible PCB or perfboard. Cut a strip ~20 mm × 60 mm. Solder the ESP32-C3 Super Mini in the center. Route I2C lines along one edge.
2
MAX30102 placement. Mount it on the underside (skin-facing side) of the band, centered on the wrist. Use a cutout in the band material above it to let the LEDs contact skin directly.
3
OLED placement. Mount on the top side, facing up, near the center of the band. Use a thin double-sided foam tape to prevent cracking the glass from flexing.
4
Battery. Attach a 400–500 mAh LiPo to the underside with double-sided tape. Route JST connector to the ESP32-C3's BAT pin. Keep away from the MAX30102 to avoid interference.
5
Strain relief. Use a small dot of hot glue or epoxy at each wire solder joint. Silicone wire (26–28 AWG) handles repeated flexing far better than standard PVC wire.
6
Conformal coat. Lightly spray the assembled PCB (except the MAX30102 optical window and USB port) with conformal coating to protect against sweat moisture.
